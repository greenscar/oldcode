The CAT SCAN Computed Axial Tomography
  • X-ray beams passed through the brain at different angles
  • Computer program computes differences in X-ray absorption in tissue to form cross-sectional images of the brain called ‘tomograms’
  • Introduced in the 1970
The PET Scan Positron Emission Tomography
  • Glucose labeled with a radioactive ‘tag’ injected into the body. Metabolism of glucose is charted as regions are activated.
  • Areas with the highest activity appear as red and yellow with green and blue showing lowest activity.
  • Introduced 1970s
The fMRI ‘Functional Magnetic Resonance Imaging’
  • Based upon increased blood flow as various parts of the brain become active
  • Non-invasively maps for language, memory, & motor function
  • 1990s
The SPECT Scan ‘Single Photon Emission Computed Tomography’
  • A radioactive isotope binds to a substance taken up by the cells in the brain
  • As brain activation occurs a computer reconstructs 3D images of brain activity levels
